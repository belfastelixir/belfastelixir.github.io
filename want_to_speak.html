Want to Speak?

We want to meet every two or three months. There should typically two talks. Sometimes there’ll be pizza.

To stay informed about our upcoming meetings you can keep up to date with #events or #elixir channels on Nitech. Alternativley you can subscribe to the meetup page.

Our goal is to accept any offered talk. If your talk is obviously about Elixir there’s no question about it; you can definitely give your talk. If it’s not so obviously about Elixir it is also welcome as one of the most important things you can do is see what is happening outside of our limited scope (as long as there is a hook back to Elixir). We’re quite an open-minded group, so the hook doesn’t have to be that clear. In the unlikely event that it turns out that Elixir isn’t a good home for your talk, we’ll do our best to come up with some alternative ideas for places you could give it.

Subject Matter

We are mixed-ability crowd; we have talks from first-timers just learning Elixir all the way up to people that have been using it for years. This means you can pitch your talk at any level and someone will get something out of it. It’s also a mostly technical crowd, so don’t shy away from getting into the nitty gritty of your topic. We’d prefer an in-depth discussion of a single aspect to a shallow overview of the whole thing.

Your talk should observe the code of conduct. Any talks that don’t will be stopped and the speaker asked to leave.

If you are using the projector you should favour large fonts and a high contrast colour scheme with dark text on a light background in anything you show. The default settings for most presentation applications are a safe bet, but you might have to re-configure your terminal or editor if you are using those for anything. Also note that while the projector will cope with most screen resolutions, it works best at 1024x768. Most presentation applications will automatically resize, but you might want to check your slides at that resolution.
